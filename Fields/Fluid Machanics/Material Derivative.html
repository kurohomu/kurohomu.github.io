Material Derivative

Usually we use [[Fields/Fluid Machanics/Description#Eulerian description|Eulerian description]] to represent fluid. However, if we want to calculate some value such as acceleration which have well defined on [[Fields/Fluid Machanics/Description#Lagrangian description|Lagrangian description]], we cannot differential it directly. Hence, we need find a new method to gives the Lagrangian time change in terms of Eulerian measurement.

The formula is
$$
\frac{D \phi}{D t}=\frac{\partial \phi}{\partial t}+\mathbf{V} \cdot \operatorname{grad} \phi
$$

Proof

We proof by connect two equation. The one is from definition, other is Taylor’s theorem
$$\Delta \phi=\phi\left(x+V_{x} \Delta t, y+V_{y} \Delta t, z+V_{z} \Delta t, t+\Delta t\right)-\phi(x, y, z, t)$$

$$\begin{aligned} \phi\left(x+V_{x} \Delta t, y+V_{y} \Delta t, z+V_{z} \Delta t, t+\Delta t\right)=& \phi(x, y, z, t) \ &+\Delta t\left(V_{x} \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial x}+V_{y} \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial y}+V_{z} \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial z}+\frac{\partial \phi}{\partial t}\right)+O\left((\Delta t)^{2}\right) \end{aligned}$$

Hence,
$$\Delta \phi=\Delta t\left(V_{x} \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial x}+V_{y} \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial y}+V_{z} \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial z}+\frac{\partial \phi}{\partial t}\right)+O\left((\Delta t)^{2}\right)$$

Dividing both sides of this equation by $\Delta t$ and appling the limit $\Delta t \rightarrow 0$, we have
$$
\lim {\Delta t \rightarrow 0} \frac{\Delta \phi}{\Delta t}=\frac{\partial \phi}{\partial t}+V{x} \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial x}+V_{y} \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial y}+V_{z} \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial z}=\frac{\partial \phi}{\partial t}+\mathbf{V} \cdot \operatorname{grad} \phi .
$$

Acceleration

The convective derivative $\frac{D}{D t}$ also applies to velocity. The acceleration of a fluid particle (following the particle) is
$$
\mathbf{a}=\frac{D \mathbf{V}}{D t}=\frac{\partial \mathbf{V}}{\partial t}+V_{x} \frac{\partial \mathbf{V}}{\partial x}+V_{y} \frac{\partial \mathbf{V}}{\partial y}+V_{z} \frac{\partial \mathbf{V}}{\partial z}
$$

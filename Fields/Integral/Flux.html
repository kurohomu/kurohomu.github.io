Flux

#Definition
If $\mathbf{F}(x, y, z)=f(x, y, z) \hat{\mathbf{i}}+g(x, y, z) \hat{\mathbf{j}}+h(x, y, z) \hat{\mathbf{k}}$ has continous components on the oriented surface $S$, and if $\hat{\mathbf{n}}=\hat{\mathbf{n}}(x, y, z)$ is the unit normal vector of the orientation at $(x, y, z)$, then
$$\iint_{S} \mathbf{F} \cdot \hat{\mathbf{n}} d S,$$
is called the flux integral of $\mathbf{F}$ over $S$.

It has physical interpretation. If $\mathbf{v}$ represents a velocity field in a fluid, and $\rho$ denotes the density of the fluid, then the flux integral
$$
\iint_{S} \rho \mathbf{v} \cdot \hat{\mathbf{n}} d S,
$$
gives the rate of flow of fluid across $S$ as a mass flow per unit time.

The process to calculate flux have particular pattern summering in following theorem.

#Theorem
If a surface $S$ is given by an equation of the form $z=z(x, y)$, where $z$ has continuous first order partial derivatives, and if $R$ is the projection of the surface onto the $x-y$ plane, then
$$\iint_{S} \mathbf{F} \cdot \hat{\mathbf{n}} d S=\iint_{R} \mathbf{F} \cdot\left(-\frac{\partial z}{\partial x} \hat{\mathbf{i}}-\frac{\partial z}{\partial y} \hat{\mathbf{j}}+\hat{\mathbf{k}}\right) d A$$
The other orientation formula can be generating by other formula.

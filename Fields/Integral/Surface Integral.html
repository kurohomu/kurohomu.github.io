Surface Integral

The fundamental idea is similar to [[Fields/Integral/Curve integral|curve integral]]integral, which is slice and add.

If we break the surface up into $m$ small surface areas $\delta S_{i}$ (as shown) then, the surface integral of the function $g(x, y, z)$ over $S$ is
$$
\iint_{S} g d S=\lim {m \rightarrow \infty} \sum{i=1}^{m} g_{i} \delta S_{i} .
$$

![[Fields/Integral/attachments/Screen Shot 2021-12-07 at 20.53.00.png|300]]

Calculation

We would like to calculate $\iint_{S} g d S$. The idea is to transfer to parameter representation. Hence, $dS$ is the main problem to calculate.

Projection

The first step is to squeeze $dS$ to 2D plane $dxdy$. For $x-y$ projection, we have
$$
d S=\frac{d x d y}{|\hat{\mathbf{n}} \cdot \hat{\mathbf{k}}|}
$$
![[Fields/Integral/attachments/Screen Shot 2021-12-08 at 12.01.57.png|250]]
This relation derive from
$$
d x d y=d S \cos \theta
$$
$$
\hat{\mathbf{n}} \cdot \hat{\mathbf{k}}=|\hat{\mathbf{n}}||\hat{\mathbf{k}}| \cos \theta=\cos \theta
$$
The absolute value ensure that the area value is positive.
![[Fields/Integral/attachments/Screen Shot 2021-12-08 at 12.02.30.png|250]]
Notion: To project onto a plane the surface must be lie entirely above or down it. For example, we are projecting the surface integral $\iint_{S} g d S$ onto the $x-y$ plane, we need all $\hat{\mathbf{n}} \cdot \hat{\mathbf{k}}>0$ or $\hat{\mathbf{n}} \cdot \hat{\mathbf{k}}<0$. To illustrate this point, consider integrating over the surface of a sphere. In order to conduct the surface integral we would have to consider the top and bottom hemispherical surfaces separately, i.e., to evaluate the surface integral we would have to split it up for all $\hat{\mathbf{n}} \cdot \hat{\mathbf{k}}>0$ and $\hat{\mathbf{n}} \cdot \hat{\mathbf{k}}<0$.

Normal vector

To calculate the unit normal vector, use the formula $z-z(x, y)=0$, which set the desire direction to coefficient 1. And we solve [[Fields/Fields/Gradient | gradient]] and normalize it. Before calculate the normal vector, choose a particular direction and use corresponding formula. However, the only difference between different direction is the coefficient.
$$\hat{\mathbf{n}}=\frac{\nabla G}{|\nabla G|}=\frac{-\frac{\partial z}{\partial x} \hat{\mathbf{i}}-\frac{\partial z}{\partial y} \hat{\mathbf{j}}+\hat{\mathbf{k}}}{\sqrt{\left(\frac{\partial z}{\partial x}\right)^{2}+\left(\frac{\partial z}{\partial y}\right)^{2}+1}}$$

Finally, we get the formula of $dS$
$$d S=\left[\sqrt{\left(\frac{\partial z}{\partial x}\right)^{2}+\left(\frac{\partial z}{\partial y}\right)^{2}+1}\right] d x d y$$
$$d S=\left[\sqrt{\left(\frac{\partial y}{\partial x}\right)^{2}+\left(\frac{\partial y}{\partial z}\right)^{2}+1}\right] d x d z$$
$$d S=\left[\sqrt{\left(\frac{\partial x}{\partial y}\right)^{2}+\left(\frac{\partial x}{\partial z}\right)^{2}+1}\right] d y d z$$
$$
\iint_{S} g(x, y, z) d S=\iint_{R} g(x, y, z(x, y))\left[\sqrt{\left(\frac{\partial z}{\partial x}\right)^{2}+\left(\frac{\partial z}{\partial y}\right)^{2}+1}\right] d x d y .
$$
$$
\iint_{S} g(x, y, z) d S=\iint_{R} g(x, y(x, z), z)\left[\sqrt{\left(\frac{\partial y}{\partial x}\right)^{2}+\left(\frac{\partial y}{\partial z}\right)^{2}+1}\right] d x d z
$$
$$
\iint_{S} g(x, y, z) d S=\iint_{R} g(x(y, z), y, z)\left[\sqrt{\left(\frac{\partial x}{\partial y}\right)^{2}+\left(\frac{\partial x}{\partial z}\right)^{2}+1}\right] d y d z
$$

The surface integral satisfy linear property and $\iint_{S} f d S=\iint_{S_{1}} f d S+\iint_{S_{2}} f d S$

Integration of a vector field over a surface

If we want to integral a [[Fields/Fields/Scalar and Vector Field#Vector Field|vector field]] $F$ over the surface $S$, we define the vector elemental area $dS$ where
$$d \mathbf{S}=\hat{\mathbf{n}} d S$$
and
$$\iint_{S} \mathbf{F} \cdot d \mathbf{S}=\iint_{S} \mathbf{F} \cdot \hat{\mathbf{n}} d S$$

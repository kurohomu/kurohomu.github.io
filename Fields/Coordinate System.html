Coordinate System

A coordinate system is a means by which every position in space can defined or determined uniquely.
To study a system, we need its triple, restriction, graph, constant surface, interchange. The restrictions on the values taken in the cylindrical and spherical coordinates ensure that each point, other than the origin, has a unique set of coordinates.

Cartesian system

  • triple: $(x, y, z)$
  • restriction: None
  • constant surface: parallel to plane

Cylindrical system

Cylindrical polar coordinates is extension of plane polar coordinates into the third dimension

  • triple: $(r, \theta , z)$
  • restriction: $r > 0$, $0< \theta <2\pi$
  • constant surface:
    • $r=r_0$: circular cylinder of radius $r_0$ centered on $z-axis$
    • $\theta = \theta_0$: half-plane attached along the z-axis and making an angle θ0 with the positive x-axis.
    • $z=z_0$: horizontal plane

spherical system

  • triple: $(\rho, \phi, \theta)$
    • $\rho$ is the distance from the origin to the point $P$
    • $\phi$ is the angle between the positive z-axis and the line from the origin to the point $P$
    • $\theta$ is the angle between the positive $x-axis$ and the line indicated by $r$ in the $x-y$ plane
  • restriction: $\rho > 0$, $0<\phi<\pi$, $0<\theta<2\pi$
  • constant surface:
    • $\rho=\rho_0$: sphere of radius $\rho_0$ centered at the origin
    • $\phi=\phi_0$: cone opening up $(0<\phi_0 <\pi/2)$
    • $\theta = \theta_0$: half-plane attached along the z-axis and making an angle θ0 with the positive x-axis.

Interchange

$$
\begin{array}{|c|cc|}
\hline \text { CONVERSION } & {\text { FORMULAE }} \
\hline(r, \theta, z) \rightarrow(x, y, z) & x=r \cos \theta, y=r \sin \theta, \quad z=z \
(x, y, z) \rightarrow(r, \theta, z) & r=\sqrt{x^{2}+y^{2}}, \quad \tan \theta=y / x, \quad z=z \
\hline(\rho, \phi, \theta) \rightarrow(r, \theta, z) & r=\rho \sin \phi, \theta=\theta, \quad z=\rho \cos \phi \
(r, \theta, z) \rightarrow(\rho, \phi, \theta) & \rho=\sqrt{r^{2}+z^{2}}, \quad \tan \phi=r / z, \quad \theta=\theta \
\hline(\rho, \phi, \theta) \rightarrow(x, y, z) & x=\rho \sin \phi \cos \theta, \quad y=\rho \sin \phi \sin \theta, \quad z=\rho \cos \phi \
(x, y, z) \rightarrow(\rho, \phi, \theta) & \rho=\sqrt{x^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}}, \quad \cos \phi=\frac{z}{\sqrt{x^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}}}, \quad \tan \theta=\frac{y}{x} \
\hline
\end{array}
$$

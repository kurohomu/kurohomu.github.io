Orientation

There are three definition of orientation:

  • A surface is said to be oriented if a unit normal vector is constructed at each point of the surface in such way that the vector change continuously.
  • A unit normal vector is said to be orientation of the surface if the surface is oriented.
  • The surface is said to be orientable if it can be oriented by construct a unit normal vector.

One vector have two orientation, and the orientation of surface is depend on the component sign of [[Fields/Integral/Surface Integral#Normal vector|normal vector]].

The orientation of surface is a relative concept.

For curve, we judge orientation by right hand principle.
